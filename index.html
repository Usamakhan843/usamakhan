Research

Breast Ultrasound Radiomics

Development of end-to-end radiomic pipelines for benign vs. malignant lesion differentiation in breast ultrasound. Work covers ROI extraction, preprocessing (cubic spline resampling, non-local means denoising, intensity normalisation), and multi-site heterogeneity analysis across three public datasets: BrEaST, BUID, and BUS-UCLM.

Deep Feature Descriptors for Medical Classification

Comparative evaluation of pre-trained deep feature extractors — DenseNet121, ConvNeXt-Base, and Swin Transformer V2-S — as radiomic descriptors for breast ultrasound classification. Study included an ablation over 14 augmentation strategies to identify optimal training configurations per descriptor and dataset.

Synchronized Image Augmentation

Design and implementation of ROI-preserving data augmentation in PyTorch using TripleTransform wrappers, ensuring geometric consistency across image, bounding-box mask, and ROI mask channels simultaneously.

Radiomics in Nuclear Medicine

Collaboration on the ITA-mIBG multi-centre study applying machine learning to automated differentiation in MIBG scintigraphy. Results published in the European Journal of Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging (2025).

BUS Radiomics Pipeline

End-to-end PyTorch pipeline for breast ultrasound radiomic feature extraction and classification. Includes synchronized augmentation, cubic spline resampling, non-local means denoising, and intensity normalisation. Supports BrEaST, BUID, and BUS-UCLM datasets.

Datasets

BrEaST — Breast Ultrasound Dataset

Public breast ultrasound dataset used for benign/malignant classification experiments, with lesion ROI masks and labels. Primary cohort in the augmentation ablation study.

BUID — Breast Ultrasound Image Dataset

Standard benchmark for breast ultrasound lesion analysis, used for cross-dataset evaluation and multi-site heterogeneity assessment.

BUS-UCLM

Breast ultrasound dataset from UCLM, used as the third cohort in comparative radiomic descriptor evaluation.
