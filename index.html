About

I am a researcher in medical image analysis and artificial intelligence, with a background in Electrical Engineering and a PhD in Industrial and Information Engineering from the University of Perugia, Italy, under the supervision of Prof. Francesco Bianconi.

My doctoral research centered on quantitative radiomics for breast ultrasound, covering the full pipeline from lesion ROI extraction and preprocessing (cubic spline resampling, non-local means denoising, intensity normalisation) to multi-site heterogeneity analysis and feature-based classification.

I am currently seeking postdoctoral, research, or applied AI positions in Europe or remote — particularly in medical imaging, radiomics, or clinical AI.

Research

[ RADIOMICS ]
Breast Ultrasound Radiomics

Extraction and analysis of quantitative imaging biomarkers from BUS datasets for benign/malignant lesion differentiation using handcrafted and deep features.

[ PREPROCESSING ]
Medical Image Preprocessing

Cubic spline resampling for isotropic voxel spacing, non-local means denoising, and intensity normalisation pipelines for multi-site data harmonisation.

[ AUGMENTATION ]
Synchronized Augmentation

ROI-preserving data augmentation in PyTorch using TripleTransform wrappers — ensuring geometric consistency across image, mask-box, and mask-ROI channels.

[ DEEP LEARNING ]
Transfer Learning for Medical AI

Comparative study of pre-trained deep feature descriptors (DenseNet121, ConvNeXt-B, Swin-V2-S) across multiple ultrasound datasets for classification tasks.

[ MULTI-SITE ]
Multi-Site Heterogeneity

Investigation of cross-dataset variability in radiomic features across BrEaST, BUID, and BUS-UCLM cohorts, informing ensemble and harmonisation strategies.

[ EXPLAINABILITY ]
Clinical Interpretability

Feature attribution and model explainability approaches for clinical AI, supporting transparent and defensible diagnostic decision support tools.
